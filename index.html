I am a Research Fellow at Microsoft Research, working with the AI4Code team. You can reach me via t-lakagrawal@microsoft.com or lakshya.aagrawal@gmail.com.

My work focuses on improving the quality of code generated by LLMs with respect to rich properties, aiming to enhance their reliability for software engineering tasks. My advisors are Dr. Aditya Kanade, Dr. Navin Goyal, Dr. Shuvendu Lahiri, and Dr. Sriram Rajamani.

I received my B.Tech in Computer Science and Applied Mathematics from IIIT-Delhi, graduating with with department rank 1. My thesis, "A SPARQL to Cypher Transpiler: Proposal and Initial Results," was supervised by Dr. Raghava Mutharaju.

I have also worked as a Summer@EPFL Research Fellow at EPFL's Very Large Scale Computing Laboratory where I developed streamblocks-graalvm, a CPU based runtime for CAL dataflow language, based on Truffle/GraalVM.

In addition, I was a Software Development Intern at Microsoft, where I worked on critical security components and Azure safe deployment practices. I also served as an Open Source Developer at INCF during the Google Summer of Code, where I implemented Pytranslate, a transpiler in Common Lisp.

For more details, check my website, Github, LinkedIn, or Google Scholar profiles.
